## Post 2

Post Link:

Summary:
Josh Braun discusses one of the most misunderstood concepts in sales: the difference between helping a prospect make a decision and trying to convince them to buy. He argues that top salespeople focus on understanding the buyer's situation, challenges, and goals rather than pushing a product.

Key Takeaway:
The best sales conversations are centered on curiosity and understanding, not persuasion. When prospects feel understood, trust increases and decisions become easier.

## Post 3

Post Link:
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_imagine-that-youre-an-artist-you-make-share-7472626639965880320-8ao0/

Summary:
Josh Braun uses the analogy of an artist creating and sharing their work to explain sales outreach. He highlights that many salespeople hesitate to reach out because they fear rejection or judgment. The post encourages focusing on providing value rather than worrying about how prospects might respond.

Key Takeaway:
Successful outreach comes from sharing ideas and value consistently without becoming emotionally attached to the outcome. Rejection is a normal part of the process and shouldn't stop action.
